HCF — Fast Methods
METHOD 1 (Factorization): Find common prime factors, take lowest power of each.
METHOD 2 (Division): Divide bigger by smaller, then smaller by remainder. Keep dividing until remainder = 0. Last divisor = HCF.
HCF of fractions = HCF of Numerators / LCM of Denominators
Example
HCF of 48 and 36?
48 = 2⁴×3 | 36 = 2²×3² → HCF = 2²×3 = 12
📊
LCM — Fast Methods
METHOD: Find all prime factors, take HIGHEST power of each.
LCM of fractions = LCM of Numerators / HCF of Denominators
HCF × LCM = Product of two numbers (only for 2 numbers)
Example
LCM of 12 and 18?
12=2²×3 | 18=2×3² → LCM = 2²×3² = 36

% Increase/Decrease
% Change = (Change/Original) × 100 If A is x% more than B: B is x/(100+x) × 100% less than A If A is x% less than B: B is x/(100-x) × 100% more than A
Shortcut: 20% more → multiply by 1.2
20% less → multiply by 0.8
30% more then 10% less → 1.3 × 0.9 = 1.17 = 17% net increase
🔄
Successive % Change
Net % = a + b + (ab/100) where a, b are the two percentages (use – for decrease)
Example
Price up 20% then down 10%. Net change?
20 + (-10) + (20×-10/100) = 10 - 2 = +8%
📉
Population & Depreciation
Population after n years = P × (1 + r/100)ⁿ Depreciation after n years = P × (1 - r/100)ⁿ
Fast: For small r and n=2: use successive % formula twice. Saves time.
Example
Population 10,000. Grows 10% p.a. Population after 2 years?
10,000 × 1.1 × 1.1 = 10,000 × 1.21 = 12,100

Ratio & Proportion Shortcuts

Key Ratio Rules

If a:b = m:n and b:c = p:q Then a:b:c = mp : np : nq Componendo: (a+b)/(a-b) = (c+d)/(c-d) if a/b = c/d Fourth Proportional to a, b, c = bc/a Third Proportional to a, b = b²/a Mean Proportional to a, b = √(ab)

Mixture Rule (Alligation)

Alligation Cross:
Cheaper ——— Mean ——— Dearer
Ratio = (Dearer - Mean) : (Mean - Cheaper)

Example: Mix milk at ₹20/L and ₹30/L to get ₹24/L
Ratio = (30-24):(24-20) = 6:4 = 3:2
💰
Partnership Problems
Profit share ∝ Capital × Time If A invests Ca for Ta months and B invests Cb for Tb months: Ratio of profits = Ca×Ta : Cb×Tb
Example
A invests ₹5000 for 6 months, B invests ₹4000 for 8 months. Share of ₹9400 profit?
Ratio = 5000×6 : 4000×8 = 30000:32000 = 15:16 A = 9400×15/31 = ₹4500 | B = ₹4900
Profit, Loss & Discount
Every formula you need with fast-solving tricks
💹
Core Formulas
Profit = SP - CP Loss = CP - SP Profit% = (Profit/CP) × 100 Loss% = (Loss/CP) × 100 SP = CP × (100+P%)/100 [Profit] SP = CP × (100-L%)/100 [Loss] CP = SP × 100/(100+P%) [find CP from SP] CP = SP × 100/(100-L%) [find CP from SP]
🎯
Dishonest Trader Trick
If trader uses false weight w instead of true weight W: Profit% = (W-w)/w × 100
Example
Trader uses 900g weight instead of 1kg. Profit%?
(1000-900)/900 × 100 = 100/9 = 11.11%
🔁
Two Article Trick
Sell 2 items at same price, one at x% profit, one at x% loss: Net Result = ALWAYS LOSS Loss% = x²/100
Example
Two items sold at ₹1000 each, 20% profit and 20% loss. Net?
Loss% = 20²/100 = 400/100 = 4% loss
🏷️
Discount & Marked Price
Discount = MP - SP Discount% = (Discount/MP) × 100 SP = MP × (100 - D%)/100 Successive discounts of a% and b%: Net discount = a + b - ab/100
Fast Trick: MP = SP × 100/(100-D%)
CP to MP with P% profit after D% discount:
MP/CP = (100+P%)/(100-D%)
Example
MP=₹500, 20% discount. SP?
SP = 500 × 80/100 = ₹400
📦
Buying & Selling More/Less Trick
If buy x items for ₹ and sell x items for ₹ (different amounts):
Buy n items at price of m (i.e., sell m, get n): Profit% = (n-m)/m × 100 (if n > m)
Example
Buy 10 apples for ₹100, sell 8 for ₹100. Profit%?
CP per apple=₹10, SP per apple=100/8=₹12.5 Profit% = (12.5-10)/10 × 100 = 25%
Simple & Compound Interest
Most formula-heavy topic — master these shortcuts to save time
🏦
Simple Interest
SI = P×R×T / 100 A = P + SI = P(1 + RT/100) P = SI×100/(R×T) R = SI×100/(P×T) T = SI×100/(P×R)
Trick: If SI on a sum for 3 years is ₹X and for 5 years is ₹Y, then Rate = (Y-X)/(P×2) × 100. Just find extra SI for 2 years → get principal.
📈
Compound Interest
A = P × (1 + R/100)ⁿ CI = A - P Half-yearly: A = P × (1 + R/200)²ⁿ Quarterly: A = P × (1 + R/400)⁴ⁿ
For 2 years: CI = P × [R²/10000 + 2R/100]
Difference: CI - SI (2 years) = PR²/10000 = SI × R/100
⚡

Example
Find CI on ₹10,000 at 10% for 2 years.
SI = 10000×10×2/100 = ₹2000 Diff = 10000×100/10000 = ₹100 CI = 2000 + 100 = ₹2100

Installment Problems
For equal installments x paying off P at R% SI over n years: P = x×n - SI on [x×n(n-1)/2] at rate R For CI: Each installment = A/(1+R/100) + A/(1+R/100)² + ...
Rule of 72: Time to double money at R% = 72/R years (approximate)
At 8% → doubles in ≈ 9 years
At 12% → doubles in ≈ 6 years
Time & Work + Pipes & Cisterns
Think in terms of "work done per day" — always use fractions
🔧
Time & Work — Core Approach
GOLDEN RULE: If A does work in n days → A's 1 day work = 1/n
If A and B together → (1/a + 1/b) work per day → Together complete in ab/(a+b) days
If A does in a days, B does in b days: Together = ab/(a+b) days A, B, C together = 1/(1/a + 1/b + 1/c) days If A is twice as fast as B: ratio of work = 2:1, ratio of time = 1:2
👥
Work & Wages
Wages are divided in ratio of work done (= ratio of efficiency)
Example
A does in 3 days, B in 6 days. Share of ₹90 total wage?
Efficiency ratio = 1/3 : 1/6 = 2:1 A gets = 90×2/3 = ₹60 | B gets = ₹30
🏗️
Men × Days × Hours
M₁×D₁×H₁/W₁ = M₂×D₂×H₂/W₂ M=Men, D=Days, H=Hours, W=Work
Example
6 men do work in 8 days. How many men for 4 days?
6×8 = M×4 → M = 12 men
🚰
Pipes & Cisterns
Inlet pipe fills: Work = +1/n per hour
Outlet pipe empties: Work = -1/n per hour
Net work = Sum of all (with signs)
Two inlets (a and b hours) + one outlet (c hours): Net filling rate = 1/a + 1/b - 1/c Time to fill = 1/(net rate)
Example
Pipe A fills in 6h, B fills in 8h, C empties in 12h. All open — time to fill?
Rate = 1/6 + 1/8 - 1/12 = 4/24 + 3/24 - 2/24 = 5/24 Time = 24/5 = 4.8 hours
⚡
Alternate Work Tricks
If A and B work alternately (A starts):
Find work in 2 days → how many complete pairs? Then check remaining.
If A alone takes 'a' days and A+B takes 't' days:
B alone = at/(a-t) days
Efficiency Method (LCM trick): Assume total work = LCM of all days. Then work per day = LCM/days. Fastest method for SSC!
Example (LCM method)
A=12 days, B=15 days, C=20 days. Together?
LCM(12,15,20) = 60 = Total work A/day=5, B/day=4, C/day=3 → Together=12/day Time = 60/12 = 5 days
Speed, Distance & Time + Trains + Boats
Three topics, one core idea — master all cases
🚗
Core Formula & Conversions
Speed = Distance / Time Distance = Speed × Time Time = Distance / Speed km/h → m/s: multiply by 5/18 m/s → km/h: multiply by 18/5
Quick conversions: 18 km/h = 5 m/s | 36 km/h = 10 m/s | 54 km/h = 15 m/s | 72 km/h = 20 m/s
⚖️
Average Speed
If same distance at speeds a and b: Avg Speed = 2ab/(a+b) [Harmonic Mean] If same TIME at speeds a and b: Avg Speed = (a+b)/2 [Arithmetic Mean]
Example
Go at 30 km/h, return at 20 km/h. Avg speed?
2×30×20/(30+20) = 1200/50 = 24 km/h
🏃
Relative Speed
Same direction: Relative Speed = |S₁ - S₂| Opposite direction: Relative Speed = S₁ + S₂
Example
A at 40 km/h, B at 30 km/h, same direction, 50km ahead. When does A catch?
Relative speed = 40-30 = 10 km/h Time = 50/10 = 5 hours
🚂
Trains — All Cases

Boats & Streams
U = Speed of boat in still water V = Speed of stream (current) Downstream speed = U + V Upstream speed = U - V U = (Downstream + Upstream) / 2 V = (Downstream - Upstream) / 2
Trick: If boat covers same distance upstream in time t₁ and downstream in t₂:
U:V = (t₁+t₂):(t₁-t₂)
Example
Downstream = 18 km/h, Upstream = 12 km/h. Boat & stream speed?
Boat = (18+12)/2 = 15 km/h | Stream = (18-12)/2 = 3 km/h
📍
Meeting Point Problems
Two people A(speed a) & B(speed b) start from opposite ends (dist D): Meeting time = D/(a+b) At meeting: A covers = D×a/(a+b), B covers = D×b/(a+b)
Shortcut: Ratio of distances covered = Ratio of speeds (when time is same)

Linear Equations Shortcuts
Cramer's Rule (2 equations):
a₁x + b₁y = c₁ and a₂x + b₂y = c₂
x = (c₁b₂ - c₂b₁)/(a₁b₂ - a₂b₁)
y = (a₁c₂ - a₂c₁)/(a₁b₂ - a₂b₁)
No Solution: a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ ≠ c₁/c₂
Infinite Solutions: a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ = c₁/c₂

Surds & Indices
aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ aᵐ / aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ (aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ a⁰ = 1 (a≠0) a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ aˡ/ⁿ = ⁿ√a

Rationalization Trick

To rationalize 1/(√a + √b): Multiply by (√a - √b)/(√a - √b)
Result = (√a - √b)/(a - b)
Example
Simplify 1/(√5 + √3)
×(√5-√3)/(√5-√3) = (√5-√3)/(5-3) = (√5-√3)/2

Statistics & Data Interpretation
Mean, Median, Mode + DI shortcuts for quick calculation
📊
Mean, Median, Mode

Mean (Average)

Mean = Sum of observations / n Weighted Mean = Σ(w×x) / Σw If all values increase by k: Mean increases by k If all values multiplied by k: Mean multiplies by k

Median

Arrange in order: If n is odd: Median = ((n+1)/2)th term If n is even: Median = avg of (n/2)th and (n/2+1)th Mode = most frequent value Relation: Mode = 3×Median - 2×Mean
📈
Data Interpretation Speed Tricks
For percentage questions on DI:
Always find 10% first, then build up (20%=2×10%, 5%=10%÷2, 15%=10%+5%)
For ratio questions: If A:B = x:y and total = T → A = T×x/(x+y)
% change in DI: (New - Old)/Old × 100
If old≈new, use approximation to save time

Range, Variance & Standard Deviation
Range = Maximum - Minimum Variance = Σ(x - mean)² / n Standard Deviation (SD) = √Variance SD is never negative. SD = 0 means all values are equal. If each value increased by k: SD unchanged (only mean changes) If each value multiplied by k: SD multiplies by |k|

SSC regularly tests AP/GP formulas and number pattern recognition

Arithmetic Progression (AP)
AP: a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d, ... nth term: Tₙ = a + (n-1)d Sum of n terms: Sₙ = n/2 × [2a + (n-1)d] = n/2 × (First + Last) Middle term of AP = Arithmetic Mean of extremes d = Common Difference = T₂ - T₁
Trick — 3 numbers in AP: Take them as (a-d), a, (a+d). Sum = 3a.
4 numbers in AP: Take as (a-3d), (a-d), (a+d), (a+3d). Sum = 4a.
Find nth term
AP: 3, 7, 11, 15... Find 20th term.
a=3, d=4 → T₂₀ = 3+(20-1)×4 = 3+76 = 79
Find Sum
Sum of 1+3+5+7+...+99
AP with a=1, d=2, Tₙ=99 → n=50 S = 50/2 × (1+99) = 25×100 = 2500

Important AP Shortcuts

Sum of first n natural numbers: n(n+1)/2
Sum of first n odd numbers: n² (1+3+5+...+2n-1 = n²)
Sum of first n even numbers: n(n+1) (2+4+6+...+2n = n(n+1))
📈
Geometric Progression (GP)
GP: a, ar, ar², ar³, ... nth term: Tₙ = a × rⁿ⁻¹ Sum of n terms: Sₙ = a(rⁿ-1)/(r-1) [if r≠1, r>1] = a(1-rⁿ)/(1-r) [if r<1] Sum of infinite GP (|r|<1): S∞ = a/(1-r) Common Ratio: r = T₂/T₁
Trick — 3 numbers in GP: Take them as a/r, a, ar. Product = a³.
Geometric Mean of a and b = √(ab)
Example
GP: 2, 6, 18, 54... Find 6th term.
a=2, r=3 → T₆ = 2×3⁵ = 2×243 = 486
Infinite GP
Sum of 1 + 1/2 + 1/4 + 1/8 + ... ∞
a=1, r=1/2 → S∞ = 1/(1-1/2) = 1/(1/2) = 2

Coordinate Geometry
Straight lines, distances, midpoints and slopes — SSC asks 2–4 questions
📍
Distance, Midpoint & Section Formula
Distance between (x₁,y₁) and (x₂,y₂): d = √[(x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)²] Midpoint of two points: M = ((x₁+x₂)/2 , (y₁+y₂)/2) Section formula (divides in m:n internally): P = ((mx₂+nx₁)/(m+n) , (my₂+ny₁)/(m+n)) Centroid of triangle (x₁,y₁)(x₂,y₂)(x₃,y₃): G = ((x₁+x₂+x₃)/3 , (y₁+y₂+y₃)/3)
Example
Distance between (3,4) and (7,1)?
d = √[(7-3)²+(1-4)²] = √[16+9] = √25 = 5
📏
Straight Lines — Everything
Slope (m) = (y₂-y₁)/(x₂-x₁) = tan θ Equation forms: Slope-intercept: y = mx + c (c = y-intercept) Point-slope: y-y₁ = m(x-x₁) Two-point: (y-y₁)/(y₂-y₁) = (x-x₁)/(x₂-x₁) Intercept form: x/a + y/b = 1 Parallel lines: m₁ = m₂ (same slope) Perpendicular lines: m₁ × m₂ = -1
Angle between two lines: tan θ = |m₁-m₂|/|1+m₁m₂|
Example
Line through (2,3) and (4,7). Slope and equation?
m = (7-3)/(4-2) = 4/2 = 2 y-3 = 2(x-2) → y = 2x-1
📐
Distance from a Point to a Line
Distance from point (x₁,y₁) to line ax+by+c=0: d = |ax₁+by₁+c| / √(a²+b²)
Example
Distance from (3,4) to line 3x-4y+5=0?
d = |3×3 - 4×4 + 5| / √(9+16) = |9-16+5| / 5 = |-2|/5 = 2/5

Area of Triangle with Vertices

Area = ½ |x₁(y₂-y₃) + x₂(y₃-y₁) + x₃(y₁-y₂)|
Collinear points check: Area = 0 means all 3 points lie on same line
⭕
Circles in Coordinate Geometry
Standard circle (centre at origin): x² + y² = r² General circle (centre h,k): (x-h)² + (y-k)² = r² General form: x²+y²+2gx+2fy+c = 0 Centre = (-g,-f) | Radius = √(g²+f²-c)
Example
Find centre and radius of x²+y²-6x+4y-12=0
g=-3, f=2, c=-12 Centre = (3,-2) | r = √(9+4+12) = √25 = 5

Mixtures, Alligation & Average
Alligation — the most powerful shortcut in SSC Maths
⚗️
Alligation Rule — The Cross Method
THE CROSS:

    Cheaper (c)           Dearer (d)
             \           /
               Mean (m)
             /           \
    (d-m)                 (m-c)

Ratio of quantities = (d-m) : (m-c)
Example
Mix rice at ₹15/kg and ₹20/kg to get ₹16/kg. Ratio?
(20-16) : (16-15) = 4:1 For every 4 kg cheap rice, use 1 kg costly rice
Example
Milk and water in ratio 4:1 in 25L. How much water to make 2:1?
Milk=20L, Water=5L. Let w more water added. 20/(5+w) = 2/1 → 20=10+2w → w=5L
🧪
Replacement Problems
If from a vessel of volume V containing liquid A, x litres are removed and replaced with liquid B, done n times: Remaining A = V × (1 - x/V)ⁿ Fraction of A remaining = ((V-x)/V)ⁿ
Classic SSC Problem
40L vessel of milk. 8L removed and replaced with water, 3 times. Final milk?
Milk = 40 × (1 - 8/40)³ = 40 × (4/5)³ = 40 × 64/125 = 2560/125 = 20.48 L
Ratio of milk:water finally = (V-x)ⁿ : Vⁿ - (V-x)ⁿ
📊
Average — All Formulas
Average = Sum of all terms / Number of terms Sum = Average × Number of terms Average of consecutive natural nos from a to b = (a+b)/2 Average of first n natural nos = (n+1)/2 Average of first n even nos = n+1 Average of first n odd nos = n Average of consecutive nos = middle number
Weighted Average: If group 1 (n₁ people) has avg A₁ and group 2 (n₂) has avg A₂:
Combined avg = (n₁A₁ + n₂A₂)/(n₁+n₂)
Example
Average of 5 numbers is 20. One number 30 is replaced by 10. New average?
Old sum = 5×20=100. New sum = 100-30+10=80 New avg = 80/5 = 16
⚡
Average Shortcut Tricks
If wrong value used:
Correct avg = Wrong avg + (Correct - Wrong)/n
If one number added/removed:
New sum = Old sum ± new number
New avg = New sum / New count
Cricket batting average trick:
Avg increases by x runs → total extra runs = x × (no. of innings)
Example: Avg goes from 40 to 42 after 10th innings → 10th innings score = old total + extra = 40×9 + extra
New total = 42×10=420 | Old total = 40×9=360 | 10th innings = 420-360 = 60
Age-Based Average (SSC Favourite)
Average age of class of 30 is 14. Teacher's age makes it 15. Teacher's age?
New total = 31×15=465 | Old total = 30×14=420 Teacher's age = 465-420 = 45 years
🎂
Age Problems
Present age = x Age n years ago = x - n Age n years later = x + n If A is n years older than B: A = B + n Ratio of ages: Set up equation with ratio → solve
Golden tip: Always assume present age as variable. Write equations for past/future directly.
Classic Age Problem
Ratio of A to B ages = 3:5. After 6 years ratio = 2:3. Present ages?
A=3x, B=5x → (3x+6)/(5x+6) = 2/3 9x+18 = 10x+12 → x=6 A=18, B=30
